About

Bachelor's in Interdisplinary Media Studies - University of California, Berkeley
Master's in Computer Science - Northeastern University (in progress)

My untraditional journey to Computer Science started when I was eager to automate a repetitive task when working at Amazon. I started taking coding classes at night, fell in love with Computer Science, and decided to pursue a career in Computer Science. Shortly after, I left my full-time job at Amazon and interned at NASA working in Software Engineering for a planetary rover system. Having no prior knowledge of the system’s technologies, I quickly learned how to be comfortable with the uncomfortable which led me to finish the mission control display software ahead of schedule.

This mentality of embracing the discomfort that comes with stepping outside your comfort zone has allowed me to succeed in different roles I’ve taken as a Computer Science graduate student such as organizing a COVID-19 Hackathon to help local non-profits, being a teaching assistant, spearheading a project in Mozillia’s Incubator Program, and more. My experiences have taught me that I am extremely passionate about the user-experience and am eager to apply and further cultivate my software engineering skills in full-stack positions.
